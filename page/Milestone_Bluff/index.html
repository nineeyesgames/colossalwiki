Milestone Bluff

Milestone Bluff (67°38′S68°45′W﻿ / ﻿67.633°S 68.750°W﻿ / -67.633; -68.750) is a rock-faced, snow-backed bluff rising to about 830 metres (2,720 ft) just west-southwest of Mount Liotard, in the south part of Adelaide Island, Antarctica. it was so named by the UK Antarctic Place-Names Committee in 1964 because the bluff is an important landmark on the inland route north of Adelaide station.[1]
