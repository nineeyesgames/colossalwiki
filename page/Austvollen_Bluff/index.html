Austvollen Bluff

Austvollen Bluff (72°6′S 3°48′E﻿ / ﻿72.100°S 3.800°E﻿ / -72.100; 3.800) is a steep rock bluff forming the east side of Festninga Mountain in the Mühlig-Hofmann Mountains, Queen Maud Land. It was mapped by Norwegian cartographers from surveys and from air photos by the Sixth Norwegian Antarctic Expedition (1956–60) and named "Austvollen" (the "east wall").
