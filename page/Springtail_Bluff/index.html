Springtail Bluff

Springtail Bluff (71°2′S165°12′E﻿ / ﻿71.033°S 165.200°E﻿ / -71.033; 165.200) is the steep, south-facing bluff that borders the eastern half of Mount Hemphill, in the Anare Mountains.[1][2] So named by the northern party of New Zealand Geological Survey Antarctic Expedition (NZGSAE), 1963–64, for the find of small insects (Collembola) in this location.[3]
