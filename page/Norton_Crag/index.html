Norton Crag

Norton Crag (78°21′S161°5′E﻿ / ﻿78.350°S 161.083°E﻿ / -78.350; 161.083) is a rock summit (1,250 metres (4,100 ft)) in northeastern Halfway Nunatak, near the center of the upper Skelton Glacier. It is named after William L. Norton, United States Geological Survey (USGS) cartographer, member of satellite surveying team at South Pole Station, winter party, 1991
