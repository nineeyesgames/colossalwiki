Pelias Bluff

Pelias Bluff (66°4′S 61°23′W﻿ / ﻿66.067°S 61.383°W﻿ / -66.067; -61.383Coordinates: 66°4′S 61°23′W﻿ / ﻿66.067°S 61.383°W﻿ / -66.067; -61.383) is a conspicuous rock bluff rising to more than 150 meters at the head of the inlet lying immediately west of Standring Inlet, on the north coast of Jason Peninsula in Graham Land. It was surveyed by the Falkland Islands Dependencies Survey (FIDS) in 1953.
