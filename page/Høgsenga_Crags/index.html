Høgsenga Crags

The Høgsenga Crags (71°53′S 5°23′E﻿ / ﻿71.883°S 5.383°E﻿ / -71.883; 5.383) are high rock crags which form the northern extremity of Breplogen Mountain in the Mühlig-Hofmann Mountains of Queen Maud Land, Antarctica. They were mapped from surveys and air photos by the Sixth Norwegian Antarctic Expedition (1956–60) and named Høgsenga (the high bed).[1]
