Tjuvholene Crags

Tjuvholene Crags (71°57′S4°28′E﻿ / ﻿71.950°S 4.467°E﻿ / -71.950; 4.467) is a high rock crags, 2,495 m, which form the north end of Mount Grytoyr in the Muhlig-Hofmann Mountains of Queen Maud Land. Mapped from surveys and air photos by the Norwegian Antarctic Expedition (1956–60) and named Tjuvholene (the thief's lair).
