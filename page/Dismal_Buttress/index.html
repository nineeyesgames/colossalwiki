Dismal Buttress

Dismal Buttress (85°27′S 178°42′W﻿ / ﻿85.450°S 178.700°W﻿ / -85.450; -178.700) is a mainly ice-free rock bluff, overlooking the west side of the head of Shackleton Glacier about 3 nautical miles (6 km) northwest of Roberts Massif. It was so named because of several depressing incidents experienced here by the Southern Party of the New Zealand Geological Survey Antarctic Expedition (1961–62), including the loss of Dismal, the party's only lead dog, which had to be destroyed.[1]
