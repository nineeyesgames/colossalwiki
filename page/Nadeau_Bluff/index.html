Nadeau Bluff

Nadeau Bluff (84°4′S175°9′E﻿ / ﻿84.067°S 175.150°E﻿ / -84.067; 175.150) is a mainly ice-covered bluff just southwest of Giovinco Ice Piedmont, protruding into Canyon Glacier from that glacier's east side. Named by Advisory Committee on Antarctic Names (US-ACAN) for F.A. Nadeau, Jr., a member of the support party at McMurdo Station, 1963.
