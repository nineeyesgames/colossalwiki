Ellis Bluff

Ellis Bluff (85°20′S175°35′W﻿ / ﻿85.333°S 175.583°W﻿ / -85.333; -175.583) is a rock bluff rising to 2,280 metres (7,480 ft) at the south side of the mouth of Logie Glacier, in the Cumulus Hills of Antarctica. It was named by the Advisory Committee on Antarctic Names for W. Ellis, a U.S. Navy chief air controlman during Operation Deepfreeze 1965 and 1966.[1]
